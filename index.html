🚗 Rental car vs. taxi — rental wins for groups

  • No Uber or Lyft on St. Thomas — taxi association has a monopoly. Taxis charge per person, $10–30/pp each way.
  • Plan to book 2 rental cars — Chase and Sidh as primary drivers. Splits cost and gives the group full flexibility to split up.
  • At ~$100/car/day split among the full group, rental cars beat taxis easily for getting around the island.
  • Heads up: driving is on the left side of the road in USVI (steering wheel is still on the left though).
  • You can take rental cars onto the St. John ferry — a major advantage for exploring the island freely.

What to Eat

Local Food Guide

Forget the tourist menus. These are the authentic Caribbean dishes you need to try while you're here — most found across St. Thomas and St. John.

🌾
Must Try
Fungi
The unofficial national dish of the USVI. A thick, savory cornmeal pudding cooked with okra — similar to polenta but distinctly Caribbean. Almost always served as a side with fish or stewed meat. Don't leave without trying it.
USVI StapleSide Dish
🥩
BBQ Culture
Caribbean BBQ
A whole different animal from American BBQ. Jerk-spiced chicken, ribs, and pork slow-cooked over charcoal and wood — heavy on allspice, scotch bonnet, and thyme. Found at roadside stands and beach shacks. Follow the smoke.
RoadsideLunch & Dinner
🫓
Street Food
Roti
Trinidadian influence runs deep in the USVI. Roti is a thin, flaky flatbread stuffed with curried chickpeas, potatoes, goat, or chicken — wrapped like a burrito. Quick, filling, and delicious. Common at local lunch spots and food trucks.
TrinidadianLunchStreet Food
🥟
USVI Snack
Pates (Pattes)
The Caribbean empanada. Deep-fried dough pockets stuffed with seasoned beef, fish, conch, or saltfish. Crispy outside, savory inside. Sold at bakeries and roadside stands everywhere. Grab a few for breakfast or a beach snack.
Fried DoughSnack / Breakfast
🍞
Bakery Staple
Johnny Cake & Dumb Bread
Two local bread staples. Johnny cake is a fried, slightly sweet dough — fluffy inside, golden outside. Dumb bread (also called coco bread) is denser, baked, and often used to sandwich saltfish or meat. Both are bakery staples perfect for breakfast.
Baked GoodsBreakfast
🍗
Comfort Food
Brown Stew Chicken
A Caribbean comfort food classic. Chicken slow-braised in a rich, dark sauce of caramelized sugar, scotch bonnet, allspice, and herbs until fall-off-the-bone tender. Served with rice and peas and fried plantains. Deeply satisfying.
ComfortDinner
🦴
Sunday Special
Oxtail
One of the most beloved dishes in Caribbean cooking. Braised low-and-slow until the meat falls off the bone and the collagen creates an incredibly rich, thick gravy. Served with rice, plantains, and festival bread. Weekend staple at local spots.
Weekend DishLunch & Dinner
🧊
Beach Treat
Fracos (Caribbean Snowcones)
The Caribbean answer to a snowcone — shaved ice drenched in intensely sweet, tropical fruit syrups: tamarind, passion fruit, mango, guava. Often topped with sweetened condensed milk. Found at roadside carts and beach vendors. A must on a hot day.
Beach VendorSnackSweet
🦐
Coastal Classic
Conch Fritters & Stewed Conch
Conch is everywhere in the USVI. Fritters are deep-fried bites of minced conch with peppers and spices — the perfect bar snack. Stewed conch is slow-cooked in a rich tomato and herb sauce. Both are quintessential island eating.
SeafoodAppetizer
🥭
Peak Season
Mango & Tropical Fruits
June is peak mango season in the USVI — we might just get lucky and find a mango tree somewhere on island (Sidh has two at home, though the old trees may not be fruiting anymore). Beyond mangoes, keep an eye out for soursop, tamarind, guava, passion fruit, starfruit, and sugar apple at local markets and roadside stands.
Peak Season JuneFresh & LocalMango Hunt 🔍
🥥
Island Fresh
Fresh Coconut
Nothing beats a young coconut cracked open on a hot beach day — sweet water, soft jelly inside. Found at roadside stands and beach vendors across the island. Sidh's home even has a coconut tree, though it's old enough that fruiting is a coin flip at this point.
Beach StapleRefreshing
🍹
Drink Up
The Painkiller & Bushwacker
Two signature USVI cocktails. The Painkiller: rum, coconut cream, pineapple and OJ, dusted with nutmeg — invented at the Soggy Dollar Bar in BVI. The Bushwacker: a frozen chocolate-coconut-rum milkshake that'll sneak up on you. Order both.
USVI OriginalsDrinks
💡 Local tip from Sidh: Skip the tourist-facing spots near the cruise ship docks for lunch. Look for local spots a block or two off the main drag, or follow the smell of a roadside BBQ grill. That's where the real food is.
Sun & Sand

Best Beaches

Every beach has a personality. Here's where to go depending on what you're looking for.

1
Magens Bay The Classic
A mile of perfect crescent sand backed by green hills — the island's most iconic beach. Calm, shallow water ideal for swimming. Entry fee ~$4/person. Gets busy with cruise crowds — go early (before 10am) or in the afternoon.
🏊 Swimming · 🤿 Some snorkeling · 🍹 Beach bar · 🚗 Drive or taxi
2
Lindquist Beach (Smith Bay) Hidden Gem
Arguably the most beautiful beach on the island and a local secret. No cruise ship stop. Crystal clear water in every shade of blue, pristine and quiet. Small entry fee on weekends. No vendors or loud music.
🏊 Swimming · 🤿 Excellent snorkeling · 🌿 Protected park · 🚗 Drive or taxi
3
Sapphire Beach Best Snorkeling
East End gem with some of the best shore snorkeling on the island. Wide, safe beach with water sports galore — kayaking, windsurfing, jet skiing, parasailing. Beachside restaurant and bar on site.
🤿 Excellent snorkeling · 🚤 Water sports · 🍹 Bar & restaurant · 🚗 East End
4
Coki Beach Most Lively
Next to Coral World Ocean Park — snorkeling and diving right off the beach. Lively atmosphere with vendors, chair rentals, and a reggae vibe. Great for people who want action. Watch your valuables.
🤿 Excellent snorkeling · 🎵 Lively vibe · 🦈 Dive shop on site · 🚗 Drive or taxi
5
Hull Bay Local Vibe
Hidden on the north shore, harder to find, worth the effort. Surfers come for the west end waves; calmer waters on the east end. Fish Bar is right here. Very local feel — no cruise ship crowds ever.
🏄 Surfing (west) · 🏊 Calm swimming (east) · 🍹 Fish Bar · 🚗 North Shore
1
Trunk Bay World Famous
Consistently ranked one of the best beaches in the Caribbean. National Park beach with an underwater snorkeling trail. Stunning turquoise water, soft white sand, dramatic tree-lined shore. Go early — it fills up. Entry ~$5.
🤿 Snorkel trail · 🏊 Swimming · 🌿 National Park · 🚌 Taxi or drive
2
Maho Bay See Sea Turtles
Best beach on the island to swim with sea turtles — they graze in the seagrass right off the beach. Calm, shallow water. Food trucks across the street. Beautiful and relatively easy to access.
🐢 Sea turtles · 🏊 Calm swimming · 🌮 Food trucks nearby · 🚗 20 min from Cruz Bay
3
Cinnamon Bay Longest Beach
The longest beach on St. John — great for water sports, picnics, and exploring. National Park campground here. Windier than other beaches so good for windsurfing and sailing. Spacious and less crowded than Trunk.
🏄 Water sports · 🏕️ Campground · 🌿 National Park · 🚗 North Shore Road
4
Honeymoon Beach Best Amenities
Powdery white sand accessible via a beautiful hike along the Lind Point Trail, or by water taxi. Chair and snorkel rentals, kayaks, paddleboards. Bikinis on the Beach bar serves frozen drinks and tacos.
🥾 Hike-in or water taxi · 🛶 Kayak rentals · 🍹 Beach bar · 🤿 Snorkeling
5
Hawksnest Bay Local Spot
Just 7 minutes from Cruz Bay but feels a world away. Locals' favorite — narrow beach, less crowded, beautiful water. Great for snorkeling along the rocky outcrops. Easy parking, no crowds.
🤿 Snorkeling · 🏊 Swimming · 🧘 Relaxed vibe · 🚗 7 min from Cruz Bay
Beyond the Beach

Top Attractions

The must-do experiences on each island beyond just laying on the sand.

🛍️
Shopping: Main Street, Havensight & Yacht Haven Grande
Three distinct shopping zones. Main Street in Charlotte Amalie is the classic duty-free strip — jewelry, spirits, and designer goods at up to 50% off US prices. Havensight is right at the cruise dock, more commercial but convenient. Yacht Haven Grande is upscale marina shopping — Gucci, Bulgari, Louis Vuitton — set along a beautiful waterfront promenade.
⛪
All Saints Cathedral & School
One of St. Thomas's most historic landmarks — and Sidh's alma mater. The cathedral dates back to the 1840s, built in Gothic style with stunning stained glass. The school grounds are beautiful and the campus sits right in the heart of Charlotte Amalie. A piece of real island history.
🏡
Plantation Manor — Sidh's Home
Where Sidh grew up — a beautiful manor tucked into the hills of St. Thomas with lush tropical grounds. On the property: two mango trees and a coconut tree, all old enough that they may or may not be fruiting by June. A personal stop on the tour and a genuine piece of island life beyond the tourist trail.
🏔️
Mountain Top
The highest point accessible by road on St. Thomas, at nearly 1,600 feet. The panoramic views are absolutely breathtaking — on a clear day you can see St. John, the BVI, and beyond. Mountain Top also claims to have invented the Banana Daiquiri. Stay for a drink and the view.
📸
Drake's Seat Viewpoint
Legendary panoramic lookout where Sir Francis Drake supposedly surveyed shipping routes. Best in the morning for photos before the haze sets in. One of the most photographed spots on the island — free, quick, and unmissable.
🏖️
Magens Bay
The most iconic beach on St. Thomas — a mile-long crescent of white sand backed by green hills, consistently ranked among the best beaches in the Caribbean. Calm, shallow water perfect for swimming. Go early (before 10am) to beat the cruise ship crowds. Entry ~$4/person. Beach bar, chair rentals, and kayaks on site.
🐠
Coral World Ocean Park
A unique marine observatory built right on the reef at Coki Beach. See sea turtles, sharks, stingrays, and Caribbean reef fish up close without getting wet. Sea Trek helmet dive lets you walk on the ocean floor. Snorkeling tours, touch tanks, and a living coral exhibit. Great half-day activity for the group.
🌿
Virgin Islands National Park
~60% of St. John is protected national park. Miles of hiking trails through lush forest, historic ruins, and stunning overlooks. The Reef Bay Trail is the crown jewel — leads to ancient petroglyphs and sugar mill ruins.
🐢
Snorkeling at Maho Bay
The best spot in the USVI to swim with green sea turtles in the wild. They come right up to you. Show up around 7–9am for the best turtle action before the crowd arrives. Completely free, no equipment needed beyond a mask.
🏛️
Annaberg Plantation Ruins
Well-preserved 18th-century sugar plantation ruins on the north shore. Self-guided tour through the mill, factory, and slave quarters. Haunting and beautiful. Sweeping views of the BVI from the ruins. Free to visit.
🛍️
Mongoose Junction Shopping
Charming open-air shopping center in Cruz Bay built with beautiful stone architecture and lush greenery. Local art galleries, boutiques, jewelry, and St. John Scoops ice cream. Feels like Swiss Family Robinson. Must-walk.
🥾
Ram Head Trail Hike
Moderate 2-mile round trip out to the dramatic Ram Head peninsula with spectacular 270° coastal views and a pebble beach below the cliffs. One of the most rewarding hikes in the USVI. Go in the morning before the heat.
🍹
Cruz Bay Beach Bar Scene
Cruz Bay has a compact, walkable beach town scene. Beach Bar, High Tide, and Joe's Rum Hut are all within a short walk. Bushwackers, Painkillers, live music, and the ferry parade in and out. Great first and last stop on the island.
